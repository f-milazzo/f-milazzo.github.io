Since 2021, I have been restoring and maintaining my grandad's VW 1302 SuperBeetle. This project, both fun and sentimentally valuable, has been a nice opportunity to do some hands-on dirty work.

Diving into the inner workings of the systems of this '71 car to identify and fix faults (that just never seem to end), gives me a chance to see the ingenious, simple solutions that engineers came up with 50+ years ago.

I am proud to say that I have made this car once again roadworthy. Starting from the fuel pump and tank, I replaced/fixed various components: the ignition, radio system, starter motor, sliding windows and speedometer were all in need of some love.

Future plans for this project include some much-needed bodywork and an additional sound system under the back seat.
