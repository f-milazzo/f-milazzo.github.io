This project consisted in the multisciplinary conceptual design of a mechanical press, based on commercially available options, followed by the dimensioning of the mechanical components.

My focus in this project lied on the preliminary kinematic analysis needed to set the dimensions of the actuation mechanism, and the final vibrational analysis on the whole trasmission.

Having chosen a simple crank-slider mechanism, the kinematic analysis focused on the length of the rod, given a crank radius of 150mm set by the preoduct requirements.

The goal of this analysis was to find a suitable caratheristic ratio λ = r/l that would minimize the velocity of the slider towards the end of its stroke, where it comes inot contact with the workpiece.

A ratio of λ = 1/10 was eventually selected, as it allowed for performance close to the ideal case of λ = 0, within a reasonable size.

The rod dimensioning process was aimed at maximing the precision of the pressing process, i.e. minimizing its compression under the nominal load of 250ton.
A simple, sturdy design was reached after some iterations, resulting in a total compression of 0.13mm, less than 2% of the rated tonnage point RTP = 10mm (the nominal stroke).

After additional stress analysis steps, the detail design was also carried out. Bearings where selected based on the load and predicted lifetime, and the design of the rod was adapted to facilitate assembly and maintenance.

For what concerns the vibrational analysis, the whole drive-slider trasmission was analyzed.

Inertia and stiffness parameters were computed from the CAD models and through FEM, respectively. The latter was in particoular computed by applying a unit load and measuring the displacement of the point of interest, for each component. Damping parameters were obtained by arbitrairly setting the damping ratio at 0.5%, and computing Rayleigh's parameters α and β, under the hypothesis of low damping.

The system was first analyzed without the crank-slider, to obtain linear analitycal solutions, that were used for a modal analysis. The crank slider was then reintroduced, together with the fabrication load.
In the absence of a control model for the electric motor, its angular velocity was set to be constant. The effect on the irregularity parameter i = (ω_max − ω_min)/ω_av of the farbication periodic load was studied.

This complete model was described through non-linear EOMs, which were divided between free and constrained DOFs and solved numerically

The numerical solution was used to check wether or not the initial requirements on irregularity i had been satisfied.

The aim of this multisciplinary project was to both strengthen the soft skills of the 4 participants, through groupwork and presentation, as well as experience first-hand the process of product design. Literature, ISO standards and commercial products were both analyzed and implemented, to deliver a final product that satisfied the product requirements.
